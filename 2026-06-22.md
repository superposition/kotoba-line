# Japanese Journal - 2026-06-22

Today I studied different readings of the kanji 日. The main lesson was that 日 does not have one fixed sound. It changes depending on the word, the date, or the phrase.

## Key Readings

| Kanji | Kana | Romaji | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 日 | ひ | hi | sun, day |
| 日 | にち | nichi | day |
| 日 | び | bi | day, as in weekday words |
| 日 | か | ka | date counter in some dates |
| 日本 | にほん / にっぽん | nihon / nippon | Japan |
| 本日 | ほんじつ | honjitsu | today, formal |
| 毎日 | まいにち | mainichi | every day |

## Sentences From The Lesson

| Sentence | Kana | Romaji | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 日暮里で毎日日が暮れる。 | にっぽりで まいにち ひが くれる。 | Nippori de mainichi hi ga kureru. | In Nippori, the sun sets every day. |
| 春日町でも日が暮れる。 | かすがちょうでも ひが くれる。 | Kasugachou demo hi ga kureru. | Even in Kasuga-cho, the sun sets. |
| 本日は誠にありがとうございました。 | ほんじつは まことに ありがとうございました。 | Honjitsu wa makoto ni arigatou gozaimashita. | Thank you very much for today. |

## Notes

- 日暮里 is read にっぽり, a special place-name reading.
- 春日 is read かすが, another special reading.
- 本日 is more formal than 今日.
- 日が暮れる means "the sun sets" or "it gets dark."
- The date readings from 1日 to 10日 are special and should be memorized as vocabulary.
